Supply Chain Optimization using Predictive Analytics

Supply chain management is a critical function in organizations where accurate demand forecasting plays a key role in reducing operational costs and improving customer satisfaction. Inaccurate predictions often lead to overstocking, stockouts, and inefficient inventory utilization.

In this project, supply chain operations were optimized using predictive analytics techniques. Historical sales and inventory data were collected and analyzed to understand demand patterns and supply behavior. Data preprocessing, exploratory data analysis (EDA), and feature engineering were performed to prepare the dataset.

Machine learning models such as XGBoost and LSTM were implemented and evaluated. Based on performance metrics including RMSE, MAE, and MAPE, XGBoost was selected as the final model. Hyperparameter tuning further improved model performance.

The final model forecasted demand for the next six months. These predictions were used to calculate safety stock, reorder points, and optimal order quantities, enabling efficient inventory management and better decision-making.

System Workflow Explanation

1. Historical Supply Chain Data

The project begins with collecting historical supply chain data including sales, inventory levels, damaged units, pricing, and time-based attributes. This data is used to understand demand patterns and overall supply chain behavior.

2. Data Preprocessing & Cleaning

The dataset is cleaned by handling missing values, formatting date columns, and encoding categorical variables. This ensures the data is structured and ready for analysis and modeling.

3. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

Visualizations such as histograms, line charts, and heatmaps are used to analyze demand trends, seasonality, and relationships between variables.

4. Feature Engineering

New features like month, year, and lag values are created to capture historical patterns and improve prediction accuracy.

5. Predictive Models (XGBoost / LSTM)

Both machine learning and deep learning models are implemented. XGBoost is used for structured data, while LSTM is used for time-series modeling.

6. Demand Forecasting

The trained model predicts future demand for the next six months, helping in planning inventory and supply chain decisions.

7. Model Optimization

Hyperparameter tuning is applied using RandomizedSearchCV to improve model performance and accuracy.

8. Inventory Optimization

Forecasted demand is used to calculate safety stock, reorder points, and optimal order quantities for efficient inventory management.

9. Decision Support Output

The final system provides actionable insights such as when to order and how much to order, improving operational efficiency.
